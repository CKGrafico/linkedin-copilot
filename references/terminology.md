## LinkedIn Platform Terms

### A

**ATS (Applicant Tracking System)** — The software companies use to manage job applications. When you apply through LinkedIn "Easy Apply," your application typically enters the company's ATS. Many ATS systems filter resumes before a human sees them.

**Alumni feature** — LinkedIn shows which of your connections (or 2nd-degree connections) attended the same school. Useful for sourcing warm contacts.

### C

**Company page** — A LinkedIn page representing an organization. Separate from individual employee profiles. Shows company info, job postings, posts, employee count, and growth trends.

**Connection** — A reciprocal LinkedIn relationship. Both parties must agree to connect. Connections become 1st-degree connections in each other's network.

**Connection request** — A request sent to another LinkedIn user to connect. Can include an optional note (up to 300 characters).

**Creator mode** — A LinkedIn profile setting that makes the "Follow" button the default (instead of "Connect"), and enables additional creator features like newsletters. Users with creator mode on have a different outreach approach required.

### D

**Degree of connection** — LinkedIn's proximity metric. 1st degree = direct connection. 2nd degree = connection of a connection. 3rd degree = one step further. 3rd+ = beyond 3rd degree.

### E

**Easy Apply** — A LinkedIn Jobs feature allowing users to apply to a job using their LinkedIn profile without leaving the platform. Increases application volume but does not inherently reduce application quality.

**Endorsements** — Peer acknowledgments of skills listed on a LinkedIn profile. Users can endorse each other's skills. Weak signal compared to written recommendations.

### F

**Feed** — The main LinkedIn homepage showing posts, updates, and activity from your network and beyond.

**Follow** — A one-way relationship on LinkedIn. You can follow someone without them following you back. Useful for building visibility before a connection request.

### G

**Ghost posting** — A job posting that has been listed but for which the role has already been filled, put on hold, or was never real. Common in the LinkedIn ecosystem. Identified by age, applicant count, and lack of response.

### H

**Headline** — The short text displayed under a person's name across LinkedIn (in search results, messages, and the profile header). Self-selected. Limited to 220 characters.

### I

**InMail** — LinkedIn Premium's direct messaging feature. Allows sending a message to anyone on LinkedIn regardless of connection status, subject to monthly credit limits. Credits are refunded when the recipient responds.

### L

**LinkedIn Recruiter** — A premium subscription tier aimed at talent professionals. Offers advanced search filters, InMail credits, and pipeline tracking. Some signals and filters referenced in this repo may be premium-only.

**LinkedIn Premium** — A family of subscription tiers (Career, Business, Sales Navigator, Recruiter). Unlocks features including InMail credits, profile viewer history, and advanced search filters.

### M

**Message request** — A message sent to someone you are not connected to in certain contexts (same LinkedIn Group, Open profile). Appears in their "Message requests" folder rather than the main inbox.

### N

**Network** — Your collection of 1st-degree connections. LinkedIn uses "network" broadly to also refer to 2nd and 3rd-degree connections.

### O

**Open profile** — A LinkedIn Premium feature allowing any user to message you for free via InMail without credits. Indicated by an "Open" badge on the profile.

**Open to Work** — A profile feature where a user signals they are seeking opportunities. Can be visible publicly (green "#OpenToWork" frame on profile photo) or privately (visible to recruiters only).

### P

**Profile completeness** — LinkedIn's internal metric for how fully a profile is filled out. Higher completeness improves search ranking. LinkedIn shows a "Profile Strength" indicator.

### R

**Recommendation** — A written endorsement from a 1st-degree connection, posted publicly on the recipient's profile. Stronger signal than skill endorsements.

### S

**Sales Navigator** — A premium LinkedIn subscription designed for sales prospecting. Includes advanced filters and lead lists. The search patterns in this repo are designed for standard LinkedIn or LinkedIn Premium (Career/Business), not Sales Navigator specifically.

**Search alert** — A saved search that sends notifications when new results match a query. Available in standard LinkedIn and premium tiers.

### T

**Talent insights** — A LinkedIn premium feature showing aggregate talent data for companies (skills distribution, hiring trends). Not relevant to the core user of this repo.

### W

**Who's viewed your profile** — A LinkedIn feature showing who has viewed your profile. Free users see a limited view. Premium users see the full list. Useful for identifying warm signals — a profile view before outreach indicates pre-existing awareness.
