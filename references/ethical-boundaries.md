# Reference: ethical-boundaries

## Summary

What is acceptable behavior on LinkedIn for a personal user doing sourcing, job searching, networking, and outreach. This is the guardrail reference for all agents. Every agent's Guardrails section should cite this file.

---

## Key Facts

- LinkedIn's Terms of Service prohibit scraping, automated data collection, and misrepresentation.
- Sending mass unsolicited messages (spamming) violates LinkedIn's policies and degrades your account's standing.
- Misrepresenting your identity, intentions, or affiliation in outreach is both a ToS violation and an ethical violation.
- Connection request abuse (sending hundreds of requests to strangers) can result in account restrictions.
- Respect that recipients may not respond. One follow-up is acceptable; more is not.

---

## Detailed Notes

### LinkedIn Terms of Service boundaries

The following activities are prohibited by LinkedIn's Terms of Service and must never be suggested or performed by any agent in this repo:

- **Scraping or automated data collection:** Any bulk automated extraction of LinkedIn data, including profile information, contact details, or search results.
- **Fake profiles:** Creating or using a profile that misrepresents your identity.
- **Misrepresentation:** Claiming a connection, title, company affiliation, or purpose that is untrue.
- **Spam:** Sending the same message or near-identical messages to large numbers of people.
- **Unsolicited commercial messages:** Sending unsolicited promotional content (selling a product or service) through InMail or direct messages.
- **Harvesting contact information:** Collecting email addresses or phone numbers from profiles for use outside LinkedIn without permission.

### Volume boundaries (personal use)

These are practical limits for a personal user operating in good faith. Exceeding these limits regularly risks account restrictions:

| Activity | Reasonable volume |
|---|---|
| Connection requests sent | < 20 per day, < 100 per week |
| InMail sent (premium) | Stay within your monthly credit allocation |
| Cold messages to 1st-degree connections | < 10 per day for new outreach campaigns |
| Profile views in one session | < 100 (very high view counts can trigger LinkedIn flags) |
| Search queries in one session | No hard limit, but avoid search automation tools |

### Outreach ethics

Beyond what LinkedIn prohibits, these practices reflect professional conduct:

- **One follow-up only for cold outreach.** Sending a third message to someone who has not responded is harassment, not persistence.
- **Respect opt-outs.** If someone declines to connect or asks not to be contacted, honor that immediately and permanently.
- **Be honest about your purpose.** If you are reaching out because you want a job, say so (professionally). Pretending to want their "advice" while actually angling for a job is dishonest and people can tell.
- **Do not contact someone who has blocked you** through a secondary account or any other means.
- **Do not fabricate shared context.** Do not claim to know someone's colleague, to have read their work, or to share a background that you do not actually share.

### Privacy considerations

- LinkedIn profiles are public, but the people behind them have not consented to being researched in depth. Use profile information to inform outreach — do not surveil.
- Do not aggregate and store detailed personal data about individuals beyond what is needed for a specific professional purpose.
- Do not share information about a specific person's LinkedIn profile with third parties without a clear professional reason.

### What this repo can and cannot help with

**In scope (this repo supports these):**
- Finding publicly available information about people and companies on LinkedIn
- Crafting professional, honest outreach messages
- Evaluating job postings and professional opportunities
- Planning follow-up in a professional, respectful cadence

**Out of scope (this repo will not help with these):**
- Automating LinkedIn actions
- Circumventing LinkedIn's rate limits or paywalls
- Scraping or bulk data collection
- Any form of deceptive outreach
- Contacting people who have opted out
