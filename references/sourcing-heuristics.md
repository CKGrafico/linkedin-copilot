# Reference: sourcing-heuristics

## Summary

High-signal indicators for identifying valuable contacts on LinkedIn. These heuristics help prioritize who is worth approaching, when, and why — based on observable profile and activity signals.

---

## Key Facts

- Recruiters who have posted or commented in the last 30 days are significantly more responsive to outreach than dormant accounts.
- A 2nd-degree connection with a strong mutual has roughly 3–5× higher response rate than a cold 3rd-degree contact.
- People who have changed roles in the last 3–6 months are often more open to new relationships and conversations.
- Profiles that show active engagement with their industry (posts, comments, articles) signal genuine domain investment, not just credential-holding.
- Internal recruiters (employed by the company directly) have more direct access to hiring decisions than agency/external recruiters.

---

## Detailed Notes

### Activity signals (ordered by strength)

1. **Posted original content in last 30 days** — strongest signal of active platform engagement
2. **Commented on or shared relevant posts** — active but less creator-identified
3. **Profile updated recently** (new role, new skills, new headline) — indicates active profile management
4. **Profile viewed your profile** — warm implicit signal; they may already know who you are
5. **Part of a LinkedIn Group relevant to your niche** — shared context, easier first message
6. **No activity in 90+ days** — weak signal; possible dormant account

### Role type signals for recruiters

- **Title contains "internal," "in-house," or company-name prefix:** usually an internal recruiter with direct hiring authority
- **Title contains "agency," "executive search," or firm name:** agency recruiter; can still be valuable for market intelligence and referrals
- **Title contains "talent acquisition" or "TA":** almost always internal; often has pipeline-level visibility
- **Title is just "Recruiter" at a named company:** likely internal

### Network proximity signals

- **2nd degree with a strong mutual you know well:** request an introduction before cold outreach — this is almost always worth asking
- **2nd degree with a weak mutual:** direct connection request with a brief personalized note
- **Same LinkedIn Group:** you can message directly without connecting (check group message permissions)
- **Follows you or has viewed your profile:** implicit interest signal; outreach is warm even if you have not connected

### Timing signals

- **Recently changed roles (< 6 months):** may be building a new team, open to conversations, still learning the new environment
- **Recently promoted:** good time to congratulate and open a dialogue
- **Posted about a hiring need or seeking candidates:** directly relevant — respond to the context they created
- **Company is in a growth phase** (headcount up 20%+): the recruiter or hiring manager is likely busy but motivated

### Red flags (signals that lower priority)

- Profile last active 6+ months ago
- Very sparse profile (no company details, no photo, no headline)
- No mutual connections and 3rd-degree only
- Profile shows "Open to Work" as a private or public banner (focus on sourcing, not networking with passive job seekers)
