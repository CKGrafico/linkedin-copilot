# Reference: job-search-heuristics

## Summary

What distinguishes a job posting worth pursuing from noise on LinkedIn — covering posting freshness, description quality, company legitimacy, and competitive dynamics. Use this to calibrate the opportunity-qualification skill.

---

## Key Facts

- A posting older than 45 days with no removal is a ghost posting risk. Treat it with caution.
- Job descriptions that list specific deliverables or team context are more likely to reflect a real, defined role.
- A company with 5+ active job postings across departments in the last 30 days is in genuine growth mode.
- "Easy Apply" does not indicate low competition — it lowers the friction to apply, which often increases applicant volume.
- A salary range included in the posting is a transparency signal. Companies that disclose compensation tend to have clearer hiring processes.
- Applicant count over 200 on a post less than 2 weeks old signals very competitive. Not necessarily a reason to skip — but be prepared.

## Detailed Notes

### Posting freshness

**Fresh (act now):** Posted in the last 7 days
- Lowest competition, most likely to still be in active sourcing
- Response from recruiters is fastest at this stage

**Normal (act this week):** Posted 8–30 days ago
- Standard competition, still active for most companies
- Some roles fill in 2–3 weeks; others take 2–3 months

**Aging (act with caution):** Posted 31–60 days ago
- May still be open, especially for senior or niche roles
- Check applicant count and company hiring activity for context

**Stale (skip unless strong reason):** Posted 60+ days ago
- High ghost posting risk
- If the company is still actively posting other roles and the count is low, it may still be worth an approach

### Description quality signals

**High-quality description indicators:**
- Specifies actual day-to-day work, not just "responsibilities include..."
- Names the team, department, or who the role reports to
- Lists concrete technologies, tools, or methodologies
- Includes a salary or compensation range
- Describes what success looks like in the first 3–6 months
- Has a realistic requirements list (not "5 years in a 3-year-old technology")

**Low-quality description indicators:**
- Generic boilerplate: "dynamic team," "fast-paced environment," "passionate about excellence"
- Requirements list that contradicts the stated seniority (junior title + senior requirements)
- No mention of team size, structure, or reporting
- Copy-pasted language that appears in multiple postings from the same company

### Applicant count benchmarks

| Applicant count | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| 0–25 | Very early or very niche. Strong opportunity to be a top-of-stack applicant. |
| 26–100 | Normal competitive range. Still worth applying. |
| 101–200 | Competitive. Strong fit and quick application matter. |
| 200+ | High volume. Less likely to stand out through the ATS alone. Warm path matters more. |
| Not shown | Company has disabled applicant count display. Cannot use this signal. |

Note: applicant counts include "Easy Apply" submissions which tend to be lower quality. A count of 150 with Easy Apply enabled may represent 30 serious candidates.

### Company legitimacy signals

**Legitimate, active company:**
- LinkedIn company page exists with real employees visible
- Company page has posts in the last 3 months
- Multiple employees have complete profiles linking to this company
- Company appears in news, has a real website, has real social presence

**Suspicious:**
- Company page has very few followers or employees
- Company was recently created and has no visible history
- Job description does not match the company's apparent size or business
- Multiple identical postings from the same company with slightly different titles
