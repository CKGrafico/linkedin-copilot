# Reference: company-evaluation-criteria

## Summary

How to assess a company as a potential employer or target on LinkedIn — covering headcount signals, hiring activity, leadership stability, cultural signals from public posts, and fit indicators.

---

## Key Facts

- LinkedIn headcount data is directional, not precise. It reflects employee-reported profiles, not official company records.
- A company with consistent headcount growth (10–30% YoY) is likely in a sustainable hiring mode.
- Recent funding rounds (especially Series B and C for startups) typically trigger 3–6 months of accelerated hiring.
- A company page with no posts in 3+ months is either LinkedIn-inactive or going through a quiet period — investigate further before investing time.
- The number and type of open job postings on a company page is one of the most reliable real-time hiring signals.

---

## Detailed Notes

### Headcount signals

LinkedIn shows approximate employee counts and growth trends (for companies that have it enabled):

| Signal | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| +20–30% headcount in 12 months | Strong growth; active hiring likely across teams |
| +5–15% headcount | Moderate growth; selective hiring |
| Flat (±5%) | Steady state; mostly backfill hiring |
| -5 to -15% | Some reduction; proceed with caution |
| -15%+ | Significant contraction; layoff risk |

Caveat: headcount on LinkedIn reflects profiles, not payroll. Actual numbers may differ. Use as directional guidance only.

### Job posting signals

Go to the company's LinkedIn page → Jobs tab:

**Volume:**
- 1–3 postings: likely backfill or opportunistic
- 5–10+ postings across departments: genuine growth mode
- 20+ postings: either aggressive scaling or recent restructuring (investigate further)

**Recency:**
- Most postings from last 30 days: actively hiring now
- Postings aging 60+ days without removal: possibly ghost postings or slow process

**Concentration:**
- All in one department: targeted team buildout
- Spread across departments: company-wide growth

**Role types:**
- Heavy on engineering or product: building product capacity
- Heavy on sales or customer success: scaling revenue teams (often post-funding)
- Heavy on operations or finance: professionalizing infrastructure (often Series B+)

### Company page activity

**Active signs (positive):**
- Regular posts (at least 2–4 per month)
- Posts about new hires, team culture, product launches, or industry topics
- Comments and engagement from employees on company posts
- Founder or leadership team active on their own profiles

**Inactive signs (cautious):**
- Last post more than 90 days ago
- Company page exists but has no "About" or "Life" sections
- Very low follower count for a company of their stated size

### Leadership stability signals

Search for the company's C-suite and VP-level leaders:
- **Stable (2+ years in role):** Organizational continuity; teams are likely settled
- **Recent hire (< 1 year):** May be building a new team or changing direction
- **Multiple recent departures visible:** Potential turbulence; worth digging into before committing time

### Culture signals from posts

Read the company's posts with these questions:
- Do employees comment with genuine enthusiasm, or do posts receive no engagement?
- Are the posts about real work (product, customers, team) or purely promotional?
- Is there diversity of voices in the posts, or is it all from one central marketing function?
- Does the company acknowledge challenges publicly, or only highlight wins? (Selective positivity is normal; complete absence of any honest tone is a yellow flag)

### Evaluating company fit

After gathering signals, assess on three axes:

**Strategic fit:** Is this company in the right domain for your goals? (Industry, product type, market position)

**Growth fit:** Is the company at the right stage and growth trajectory for what you are looking for? (Early-stage uncertainty vs. established stability)

**Cultural fit:** Based on available signals, does the company's public behavior align with what you value in a workplace?
