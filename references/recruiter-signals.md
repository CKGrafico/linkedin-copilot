# Reference: recruiter-signals

## Summary

Explains what recruiter activity patterns on LinkedIn mean, how to distinguish internal from external recruiters, and how to interpret company hiring signals that indicate whether a recruiter is worth approaching.

---

## Key Facts

- A recruiter with recent job postings on their company page is actively sourcing, not just maintaining a profile.
- Internal recruiters (employed by the company) can directly refer you into a process. Agency recruiters are intermediaries who may or may not have an active mandate.
- A recruiter who has posted content about hiring in a specific domain is signaling active demand in that area.
- Companies with a 10–30% headcount increase in the past year are typically in sustained hiring mode.
- A recruiter who recently joined a company (< 6 months) is often building a new function or scaling a team — this is a high-signal timing opportunity.

---

## Detailed Notes

### Internal vs. agency recruiter signals

**Internal recruiter indicators:**
- Job title lists the company name (e.g., "Recruiter at Stripe" or "Senior TA Partner | Stripe")
- Profile shows only one employer — the target company
- LinkedIn page association shows the target company as the current employer
- Title variants: "Talent Acquisition," "TA Partner," "People Partner," "Recruiting Manager"

**Agency / external recruiter indicators:**
- Title mentions "Search," "Executive Search," "Staffing," or a firm name (e.g., "Heidrick & Struggles")
- Profile shows multiple client engagements or a firm as employer
- Bio mentions "I recruit for clients in [domain]" or similar framing

**Why it matters:**
An internal recruiter can put your application directly in front of a hiring manager. An agency recruiter can be useful for market intelligence and passive introductions, but they need an active mandate to be immediately actionable.

### Recruiter activity signals (ordered by strength)

1. **Posted job openings in the last 2 weeks** — actively sourcing; directly relevant
2. **Posted content about team culture, new hires, or "we're hiring"** — signals active hiring mode
3. **Engaged with candidate posts or replied to public applications** — responsive and active
4. **Recently joined the company** (< 6 months) — often means new team or scaling function
5. **Posted about a specific role or domain** — direct signal of what they are actively filling
6. **Profile updated recently** — maintains an active LinkedIn presence
7. **No posts in 6+ months** — lower priority; account may be passive

### Company hiring signal patterns

**Strong hiring signals:**
- LinkedIn headcount up 15%+ year-over-year
- Multiple new job postings across departments in the last 30 days
- Recent funding announcement (Series A/B/C: often triggers team growth)
- Company recently posted "we're growing" or similar content
- Leadership team has been stable (low turnover suggests organizational health)

**Ambiguous signals:**
- Single job posting with no other activity (could be a backfill or one-off)
- Company page present but no posts (may just not use LinkedIn for content)

**Caution signals:**
- Headcount flat or declining
- Multiple recent departures visible in connection feed
- Job postings posted repeatedly over 3+ months without being removed (possibly ghost posts)
- Sudden spike in all-department postings (may indicate recent mass layoff and rebuild)

### Reading a recruiter's posting history

When viewing a recruiter's profile, check their "Activity" section:
- Are the most recent posts within 30 days?
- Do they mention specific roles they are filling?
- Do they post about culture, team wins, or company milestones? (Engagement with the company beyond just sourcing)
- Do their posts receive engagement from their own company's employees? (Healthy culture signal)
